Content Production at Rooster Teeth Productions

About Rooster Teeth Productions

Rooster Teeth is a production studio based in Austin, TX responsible for some of the most watched and celebrated online series ever. The company ignited an Internet revolution and helped redefine contemporary animation when it created Red vs. Blue, longest-running web and American science fiction series in history. Rooster Teeth also produces the globally acclaimed animated show RWBY, the first western anime series to be distributed in Japan, the comedy gamer series Immersion, a reality format that brings video game theory to the real world, the chart-topping RT Podcast, and hilarious live action shorts such as Million Dollars, But… and Social Disorder. With a massive global footprint of more than 20 million subscribers to its YouTube network, 3 million unique monthly visitors to RoosterTeeth.com and 1.8 million registered community members, Rooster Teeth has one of the most active and engaged fan bases in the world. In 2011, Rooster Teeth expanded into live events with RTX, an annual gaming and Internet convention held in Austin, TX, which started as a fan event for 400 people but quickly grew to attract more than 40,000 attendees from around the world in 2015. Rooster Teeth launched its first international live event in January 2016 with RTX Australia. The company broke Indiegogo crowd funding records after rallying its dedicated fan base for Rooster Teeth's first feature film, Lazer Team, which raised $2.48M. In addition Rooster Teeth operates the popular online gaming destinations Achievement Hunter, Funhaus, The Slo Mo Guys, Kinda Funny, Cow Chop, The Know, and ScrewAttack. In November of 2014, Rooster Teeth became a subsidiary of Fullscreen, Inc., a global youth media company that develops online creators and produces multi-platform entertainment experiences.

History of Rooster Teeth Productions

Rooster Teeth Productions was founded on April 1, 2003 by Matt Hullum, Burnie Burns, Geoff Ramsey, Gus Sorola, and Joel Heyman. Rooster Teeth’s first huge success began after the creation of the longest-running webseries of all time, Red vs. Blue, which premiered in April 2003. This ignited an Internet revolution and helped redefine contemporary animation. Rooster Teeth’s next big success was their following animated series, RWBY. One of the founders of Rooster Teeth Productions, Geoff Ramsey, created a division within Rooster Teeth, named Achievement Hunter in 2008. Geoff constructed a team of gamers and entertainers to make up Achievement Hunter at the time, and later on they would triple in numbers. This was their first division made within Rooster Teeth and it was the go to spot for gaming related things under Rooster Teeth on the Internet. Overtime, more and more divisions were created and this is what makes Rooster Teeth unique compared to other related online gaming related outlets. For example, a team working at Inside Gaming, a division within Machinima, left the company and was brought on by Rooster Teeth to create their next huge division, Funhaus. Each division at Rooster Teeth has their own little niche to satisfy the different needs content consumers need on the Internet. Rooster Teeth since then has been adding onto their diverse cast of teams and members to construct a beautiful company with many different flavors for their audience to watch, love, and relate with.

Job Description

Rooster Teeth Productions is looking for a Video Production Intern to become a part of their Funhaus team in Los Angeles, CA. I will be exposed to all areas of the business via a solid foundation of learning through training and work experience. They are looking for fun, energetic, skilled college students who want to learn the ins and outs of online video production and post-production through the use of the Adobe Creative Suite. They also have the need for production assistance on studio and field productions with both live action and video game gameplay. This could include live sound recording, studio camera work, and production assistance, as well as general help in maintaining equipment in the studio. This internship is available to students who are currently enrolled in a film program in college/university. I will be required to get school credit. They record 14 episodes of their various shows a week. In the last month, these broadcasts have accumulated over 25 million views across youtube (excluding their website views). They use the 2015 Adobe Creative Suite to utilize Photoshop, Premiere, and After Effects for our post-production needs. They also currently utilize the latest in professional video equipment including a Elgato capture devices, Alesis audio mixers, Canon DSLRs, and Audio-Technica standing microphones.

Job Responsibilities and Qualifications

I must have the ability to create comedic Adobe Photoshops for videos, convert video, music, and image files into formats suitable for editing, use Adobe Premiere to sync/edit videos, gather assets for the original programming (video files, pictures, music, etc.), and complete various office duties. I must be currently enrolled in college and taking the internship for course credit (I must provide a letter of verification), be in the Los Angeles area, have experience in Adobe Creative Suite (Premiere CC 2015, Audition, Photoshop, etc.) and Google Docs, have some knowledge of video games (retro to current gen.), have good understanding of navigating the internet (social media savvy), be a team player, be able to meet deadlines, and be hardworking and driven.
